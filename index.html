What

dhgetdown is a one-day conference on digital humanities. The aims of the conference are to highlight on-going digital humanities work in Hawaiʻi and beyond and to energize the local community of scholars, educators, artists, and others interested in incorporating digital humanities tools and methods into their work.

What can you expect?

  • Hear our keynote panelists – Dr. Noelani Arista (History), Jeanette Hall (American Studies), and Dr. kuʻualoha hoʻomanawanui (English) – discuss their experience with digital humanities at UH Mānoa.
  • Participate in a hands-on digital mapping workshop and contribute to a collaborative storytelling project.
  • Learn about our favorite digital mapping projects and resources for embarking on your own.
  • Connect with other participants and grow the local digital humanities community of practice.

Everyone, novice or expert, is welcome to attend. We all have a lot to learn from each other!
